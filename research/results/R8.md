# Validating a 7-Register Fund-Type Taxonomy for a Tokenized-Equity NAV / Creation-Redemption Protocol

## TL;DR
- **The 7-register count is essentially correct but should be refined to 7 core registers plus 2–3 orthogonal capability layers.** L1, L2, L4, L5, L6 and L7 are genuine phase transitions; L3 should be SPLIT into L3a (scheduled reconstitution) and L3b (threshold reweight) because they are different builds; and "NAV-strike batching/swing pricing" plus "interval/gated redemption" are sub-modules of L5/L6, not new levels.
- **The defensible wedge is L4 and L6 (the weekend registers).** TradFi has no genuine 24/7 binding-valuation analog because markets close; the only real-world on-chain precedent for closed-market trading is Solana xStocks, where weekend volume drops sharply and thin pools make honest closed-market NAV the scarce primitive.
- **Build order by demand × inverse-complexity:** L1 → L2 → L4 (read-only fair value) form the MVP; L5 (forward-priced create/redeem) is the first settlement-binding build; L3a/L3b and L6 follow; L7 (leverage/derivatives) is last. Cross-asset collateral (B1) is an integration, not a build; corporate actions (B2) and cash component (B3) are orthogonal capabilities needed from L2 onward.

## Key Findings

1. **The single ordering axis holds.** Ordering by "how much the product depends on a price oracle and how binding that price is" produces genuine phase transitions: no oracle (L1) → display oracle (L2) → oracle-triggered open-market trades (L3) → 24/7 informational oracle (L4) → oracle-as-settlement (L5) → 24/7 binding/forced (L6) → continuous path-dependent (L7). Each step forces a new on-chain module.

2. **One level should split, none should merge upward.** "Scheduled reconstitution" (S&P 500-style, calendar-driven, listing-gated) and "threshold reweight" (equal-weight/vol-target, math-driven) are genuinely different builds — different triggers, different correctness risks. Cap-weighted hold (L2) is NOT build-identical to a frozen bundle (L1): the frozen bundle never needs the oracle to be binding, whereas a self-tracking cap-weighted basket needs a display oracle and corporate-action handling. These are correctly separated.

3. **TradFi's closed-market valuation is a solved, regulated problem — and it is informational, never settlement-binding overnight.** Mutual funds forward-price once daily at 4pm ET (Rule 22c-1); international funds fair-value stale foreign closes using third-party models (ICE FVIS); ETFs reconcile via in-kind creation/redemption and authorized-participant arbitrage (Rule 6c-11). Crucially, **no TradFi product strikes a binding price while its reference market is closed.** That is the white space.

4. **On-chain precedents cluster at the extremes and have a graveyard in the middle.** Static/index baskets (Reserve DTFs, Index Coop, Symmetry, EqualFi) and synthetic 24/7 equities (Mirror, Synthetix) exist; the synthetic-equity register is a graveyard (Mirror dead since 2022 after the Terra/UST collapse and Band oracle withdrawal; Synthetix delisted nearly all synths after SNX fell 94% between February 2021 and June 2022, leaving only synthetic Ether and Euro). The honestly-backed 24/7 register is young — but it is now scaling fast: Ondo Global Markets crossed $1 billion TVL in under eight months (May 2026), holding >70% of the tokenized-equity issuer market.

5. **The weekend gap is real.** On-chain equity volume drops materially on weekends; tokenized stocks can trade at "ghost" prices when the reference market gaps; and DEX depth is thin beyond top tickers. This is precisely the risk our L4/L6 modules address, and TradFi has no equivalent because its markets simply close.

## Details

### Master Table (Rows L1–L7)

**L1 — Static in-kind, NO oracle**
- *Representative fiat product:* Unit Investment Trust (UIT) / defined portfolio. UITs hold a fixed, unmanaged portfolio until a set termination date; the SEC defines them as one of the three types of investment company, holding "a relatively fixed portfolio of securities… with little or no change for the life of the UIT." First Trust and Invesco are major UIT sponsors.
- *AUM + date:* UIT assets are a small slice of the fund universe (low hundreds of billions, ICI data); individual equity UITs are small.
- *Actions & cadence:* One-time deposit at creation; buy-and-hold; no rebalance; redeemable at NAV; dissolves at termination (13 months to 30+ years).
- *TradFi valuation:* Daily NAV from market closes for display/redemption only; value = backing.
- *Closed-market behaviour:* Not revalued; value is the fixed quantity of backing units.
- *On-chain precedents + failures:* Reserve Index Protocol DTFs (explicitly oracle-free: "designed to operate without needing to know any prices of any collateral assets"); EqualFi EqualIndex (static, deterministic, "no rebalancing, no changing weights… bundle stays fixed forever"). No notable de-pegs because no binding price.
- *Our new module:* In-kind vault + factory (the spine). 1:N mint/burn against fixed quantities.
- *Dominant risk:* Custody/backing correctness and redemption-gating bugs; minimal oracle risk.

**L2 — Read-only NAV, market-hours only**
- *Representative fiat product:* Cap-weighted index funds — Vanguard S&P 500 ETF (VOO), SPDR S&P 500 ETF (SPY), Vanguard Total Stock Market (VTI).
- *AUM + date:* VOO became the first ETF to surpass $1 trillion AUM on June 2, 2026 (confirmed by Morningstar and Bloomberg); the same day, iShares Core S&P 500 (IVV) ended at just under $861 billion and SPY at almost $786 billion.
- *Actions & cadence:* Holdings self-track by price; weight drift is automatic; NAV struck daily; periodic share-count updates. No active reweighting between index events.
- *TradFi valuation:* Fund accounting agent computes NAV at 4pm ET from closing prices via pricing services (ICI).
- *Closed-market behaviour:* Uses last close; stale overnight/weekend.
- *On-chain precedents + failures:* Index Coop DPI (cap-weighted, NAV via component oracles), Symmetry vaults on Solana (Pyth-priced, 350+ community baskets). Index Coop's earlier "take DEX liquidity" rebalances caused NAV drag — fixed via Dutch auctions.
- *Our new module:* Display NAV oracle — Σ qty·price + staleness flag + market-status flag.
- *Dominant risk:* Oracle staleness/manipulation; mis-signalling NAV as binding when it is display-only.

**L3 — Oracle-driven rebalance, market-hours only (SPLIT into L3a / L3b)**
- *L3a Scheduled reconstitution:* S&P 500 / Russell. S&P 500 rebalances quarterly after the close of the third Friday of March/June/September/December; reference date is five weeks prior; ad-hoc changes for M&A/bankruptcy with ≥3 business days' notice. FTSE Russell is moving to semi-annual (June and November) in 2026. The September 2025 rebalance was "the busiest quarterly rebalancing in four years, affecting nearly $250 billion worth of stocks, according to Piper Sandler" — and coincided with the expiration of equity options worth more than $5 trillion (CME Group OpenMarkets); it added APP, HOOD and EME and removed MKTX, CZR and ENPH.
- *L3b Threshold reweight:* Invesco S&P 500 Equal Weight ETF (RSP) resets each constituent to 1/N quarterly (after the close of the third Friday of the quarter-ending month, per S&P methodology); vol-target and tactical funds rebalance on threshold breach.
- *AUM + date:* RSP is a multi-tens-of-billions equal-weight ETF (Invesco, 2026).
- *Actions & cadence:* L3a = calendar + listing-gate + committee discretion; L3b = math rule on a schedule or trigger.
- *TradFi valuation:* Index provider computes; funds trade at close on effective date to minimize tracking error.
- *Closed-market behaviour:* Trades happen at official close only; never while closed.
- *On-chain precedents + failures:* Index Coop monthly reconstitution (determination week + first-business-day reconstitution); Reserve Index DTF Dutch-auction rebalances; Symmetry keeper-run Dutch auctions with 3% max slippage vs Pyth oracle.
- *Our new module:* Rebalance engine with sub-modules — reweight-to-target (L3b) vs reconstitution + listing gate + committee/calendar trigger (L3a).
- *Dominant risk:* L3a = listing/corporate-action correctness and front-running the known effective date; L3b = oracle manipulation at the trigger and rebalance slippage.

**L4 — 24/7 read-only fair-value NAV (the weekend gap; informational)**
- *Representative fiat product:* International-fund fair-value pricing (ICE Fair Value Information Services); deprecated iNAV/IIV for ETFs.
- *AUM + date:* Fair-value pricing is used by nearly all international mutual funds (MSCI research bulletin); ICE FVIS is an industry-standard utility for listed equities, equity index futures and international bonds.
- *Actions & cadence:* Continuous/triggered fair-value estimate when the reference market is closed; informational only.
- *TradFi valuation:* Third-party multi-factor models (ICE/Interactive Data) estimate "a price that would likely prevail in a liquid market," using open-market signals (futures, ADRs, FX, betas) to adjust stale closes.
- *Closed-market behaviour:* THIS IS the closed-market behaviour — fair-valued, not stale, not binding.
- *On-chain precedents + failures:* Solana xStocks is the only live 24/7 self-custody tokenized-equity precedent (Backed Finance, launched June 30, 2025; acquired by Kraken Dec 2025; Chainlink price feeds + Proof-of-Reserve). RedStone has publicly flagged the "weekend gap" / "ghost price" risk. Kraken states that outside market hours, market makers approximate fair value via ATS platforms, index futures, and internal models.
- *Our new module:* Closed-market fair-value model — lastClose·(1+Σβ·signal) + confidence band + off-chain beta attestation + market-status oracle.
- *Dominant risk:* Weekend liquidity/price-gap risk; beta-model staleness; signalling fair value as binding.

**L5 — Oracle-as-settlement: forward-priced cash create/redeem**
- *Representative fiat product:* Open-end mutual funds (Rule 22c-1 forward pricing); cash-create ETFs.
- *AUM + date:* US open-end mutual funds are tens of trillions (ICI Fact Book).
- *Actions & cadence:* Orders queue intraday; all transact at the next-computed NAV (typically 4pm ET); forward pricing prevents late-trading dilution.
- *TradFi valuation:* NAV next-computed after order receipt is binding for settlement; this is the first register where the price binds.
- *Closed-market behaviour:* Orders received after cutoff get the next business day's NAV — i.e. the queue waits for the market to open. Section 22(e) exceptions allow delay when the NYSE is closed beyond normal weekends.
- *On-chain precedents + failures:* Ondo Global Markets mints/redeems 24/5 (Sun 8:05pm–Fri 7:59pm ET) — effectively a forward-priced window keyed to market hours. Ondo crossed **$1 billion TVL in under eight months since its September 2025 launch** (CCN/Yahoo Finance, May 11, 2026, citing RWA.xyz), holding **more than 70% of the tokenized-equity issuer market** with **over $18 billion in cumulative trading volume** across Solana, Ethereum and BNB Chain (260+ tokenized US stocks/ETFs). Backed/xStocks redemption at NAV only for KYC'd participants.
- *Our new module:* Forward-priced queue + settle-at-next-open + replay protection.
- *Dominant risk:* Replay/late-trading exploits; defining "next open" deterministically on-chain; oracle becomes settlement-binding.

**L6 — 24/7 binding rebalance / forced redemption**
- *Representative fiat product:* ≈ none in TradFi (markets closed); closest analogs are vol-target funds and lending-protocol liquidations.
- *AUM + date:* N/A — no TradFi product binds a price while its reference market is closed.
- *Actions & cadence:* Continuous monitoring; forced liquidation/rebalance on trigger regardless of US market status.
- *TradFi valuation:* No analog. Lending liquidations (Aave/Kamino) are the closest 24/7 binding mechanism.
- *Closed-market behaviour:* This register exists ONLY on-chain. Kamino already liquidates xStock collateral 24/7; weekend price gaps make this dangerous.
- *On-chain precedents + failures:* Mirror Protocol minting-liquidations and Synthetix debt-pool liquidations (both dead/curtailed); Kamino lending against xStocks; Index Coop / Reserve Dutch-auction liquidation patterns.
- *Our new module:* Buffered-trigger guard + listing gate + Dutch-auction liquidation + sequencer-downtime mitigation.
- *Dominant risk:* Sequencer downtime during a forced action; weekend illiquidity causing liquidation cascades; oracle manipulation triggering false liquidations.

**L7 — Leverage / derivatives / structured (continuous, path-dependent)**
- *Representative fiat product:* Leveraged/inverse ETFs (ProShares UltraPro QQQ, TQQQ); defined-outcome/buffer ETFs (Innovator); structured notes; managed futures.
- *AUM + date:* TQQQ ~$36B AUM, 0.82% expense ratio (May 2026, aggregator); Innovator Defined Outcome suite >$29B AUM across 150+ funds as of Oct 31, 2025 (Innovator).
- *Actions & cadence:* TQQQ rebalances derivative exposure DAILY to reset 3x; buffer ETFs hold FLEX options and roll annually on the last business day of the outcome month.
- *TradFi valuation:* Daily NAV; path-dependence from daily reset causes volatility decay; FLEX options valued via OCC settlement.
- *Closed-market behaviour:* Repriced at next close; daily reset is the binding event.
- *On-chain precedents + failures:* Toros/dHEDGE leveraged tokens (Aave-based looping, rebalance only when leverage drifts outside e.g. 2.8x–3.2x band, "no forced liquidation"); Index Coop leverage tokens / Smart Loops (up to 15x via Aave/Morpho). Volatility decay is the documented drag.
- *Our new module:* Derivative legs + funding accounting + continuous/daily rebalance + risk caps.
- *Dominant risk:* Path-dependence/volatility decay; funding-rate accounting; continuous rebalance compounding errors; liquidation under leverage.

### Merged / Split / Missing Levels (corrected count)

**SPLIT — L3 → L3a + L3b.** Scheduled reconstitution and threshold reweight are different builds. L3a needs a listing gate, an effective-date calendar, and committee/eligibility logic (correctness risk = corporate actions and index membership). L3b needs a math rule and a trigger evaluator (correctness risk = oracle at the trigger). Collapsing them hides a real module boundary.

**CONFIRMED DISTINCT — L1 vs L2.** A frozen bundle (L1) never makes the oracle binding and needs no reweighting. A cap-weighted self-tracking basket (L2) needs a display oracle, staleness handling, and corporate-action capability. Different builds; keep separate.

**Checked and assigned (cross-cutting Q6):**
- *NAV-strike batching / swing pricing / anti-dilution levies* → sub-module of L5. Swing pricing (Rule 22c-1, 2016) adjusts NAV by a swing factor past a threshold; notably, no US fund has implemented it. On-chain, this is an anti-dilution add-on to the forward-priced queue, not a new level.
- *ADR & FX parity baskets / multi-currency NAV* → sub-module of L2/L4 (an FX leg on the display/fair-value oracle). Not a new level, but a real capability.
- *Semi-transparent active ETFs (ActiveShares / proxy basket)* → sub-module of L3/L5. Mechanism = proxy/tracking basket + verified intraday value (VIIV every 1 second for ActiveShares vs 15s IIV) so APs can arbitrage without seeing true holdings. On-chain this is a "shielded basket" variant — interesting but not a phase transition on the oracle-binding axis.
- *Interval funds / gated redemption (Rule 23c-3)* → sub-module of L5/L6. Repurchase offers at 3/6/12-month intervals, 5–25% of shares, NAV struck on a repurchase pricing date ≤14 days after the request deadline, pro-rata if oversubscribed. On-chain this is a redemption-gating module on the forward-priced queue.
- *Commodity / futures-roll funds* → belongs in L7 (path-dependent roll) or as an L3b variant if calendar-rolled. The roll schedule is a derivative-leg cadence.

**Net corrected count: 7 core registers (with L3 split into 3a/3b → effectively 8 build-distinct modules) + 3 orthogonal capability layers (B1 collateral integration, B2 corporate actions, B3 cash component).**

**Side branches (confirm NOT on linear axis):**
- *B1 cross-asset collateral* — CONFIRMED an integration, not a level. Using a basket NAV as binding collateral in a lending market is a consumer of L4+ NAV (Kamino already does this with xStocks). It is the most dangerous consumer because it makes our informational NAV someone else's liquidation trigger.
- *B2 corporate actions (splits/dividends)* — CONFIRMED orthogonal; needed from L2–L3 onward. xStocks handles this via rebasing (balance adjusts for splits/dividends); Ondo via total-return reinvestment (token count increases). This is a capability every register above L1 needs.
- *B3 cash component / fractional balancing* — CONFIRMED orthogonal; a balancing-amount module used across L3–L7 (mirrors the ETF "cash balancing amount" in Rule 6c-11).

### TradFi boundary (Q8)
- *On-chain genuinely exceeds TradFi:* 24/7 trading and settlement; atomic in-kind creation/redemption; permissionless arbitrage; sub-second, sub-cent settlement (Solana); composability (a tokenized stock is instantly collateral). xStocks settle in 30s–2min vs T+1.
- *TradFi still ahead:* Corporate-action correctness (transfer-agent rails, dividend withholding, proxy voting); deep liquidity — per Tiger Research, "US equity markets turn over about 1.1 trillion dollars a day. Stock perp open interest, the total of contracts currently live, sits at 2.25 billion dollars… the market is clearly early stage" (Q1 2026); regulated fair-value infrastructure (ICE FVIS); investor protections (Rule 22c-1 anti-dilution, Rule 6c-11 disclosure). Tokenized stocks confer no shareholder rights and (mostly) no direct dividends.

## Recommendations

**Staged build order (demand × inverse-complexity):**

1. **MVP cut-line: L1 → L2 → L4.** Ship the in-kind vault + factory (L1, lowest audit surface, the spine), add the display NAV oracle (L2, high demand — every index product needs it), then the closed-market fair-value NAV (L4). L4 is moderate complexity but it is the wedge: it is the scarce primitive no one ships honestly, and it is read-only (informational), so a model error embarrasses but does not directly liquidate anyone. Build L4 as informational-only first.

2. **First settlement-binding build: L5.** Forward-priced create/redeem keyed to next-open. This is where the oracle first binds; demand is high (Ondo's run from launch to $1B TVL in under eight months proves the 24/5 mint/redeem market). Pair it with B2 (corporate actions) and B3 (cash balancing), which are prerequisites.

3. **Then L3a/L3b (rebalance engine) and L6 (24/7 binding).** L3 is medium demand, medium complexity; L6 is the second half of the wedge but the highest risk (sequencer downtime + weekend illiquidity + binding price). Gate L6 behind buffered triggers and Dutch-auction liquidation, and treat sequencer-downtime mitigation as a hard prerequisite.

4. **Defer L7 (leverage/derivatives).** Highest audit surface, path-dependence, funding accounting; smaller initial issuer demand; let Toros/Index Coop patterns mature.

**Benchmarks that would change the order:**
- If a lending market (Kamino-style) commits to consuming our NAV as binding collateral (B1), pull L4's confidence-band and L6's buffered-trigger work forward — the informational NAV becomes a liquidation trigger and must be hardened first.
- If weekend on-chain equity volume rises materially toward weekday levels, L6 demand rises and its liquidity risk falls — accelerate it.
- If Robinhood Chain mainnet launches with live tokenized-equity tickers (testnet today uses synthetic stock tokens; Chainlink is the default oracle), prioritize the L5 forward-priced queue to capture issuer integrations at launch.

**The wedge is L4 + L6 (weekend registers), confirmed.** L4 is the honest 24/7 informational NAV; L6 is the honest 24/7 binding action. TradFi cannot do either (markets close); the only on-chain precedent (xStocks on Solana) demonstrates the demand and the unsolved liquidity/price-gap problem.

## Implications for Our Architecture
- **Design first:** the in-kind vault + factory (L1 spine), the display NAV oracle with staleness + market-status flags (L2), and the closed-market fair-value model with confidence bands + off-chain beta attestation (L4). These three are the MVP and contain the wedge.
- **Design next, as binding modules:** the forward-priced queue with replay protection (L5), plus the corporate-action engine (B2) and cash-balancing module (B3) as orthogonal capabilities that L5+ depends on.
- **Defer:** the rebalance engine split into reconstitution+listing-gate (L3a) and threshold-reweight (L3b); the 24/7 buffered-trigger / Dutch-auction / sequencer-mitigation guard (L6); and the derivative-leg/funding/risk-cap stack (L7).
- **Integrations, not builds:** cross-asset collateral (B1) — design the NAV oracle's confidence band and staleness semantics assuming a lending market will consume it as a liquidation trigger, but do not build the lending market.
- **Venue note (neutral):** Robinhood Chain (Arbitrum Orbit L2, Chainlink default oracle, mainnet expected later 2026) is the deployment target; its testnet stock tokens are synthetic, so anchor all closed-market liquidity assumptions on Solana xStocks data, not RHC.

## Caveats
- **AUM/TVL figures carry as-of dates and some are aggregator cross-checks.** VOO's $1T (June 2, 2026, primary: Morningstar/Bloomberg), Innovator's $29B (Oct 31, 2025), TQQQ's ~$36B (May 2026 aggregator), and Ondo's $1B TVL (May 11, 2026, RWA.xyz via CCN) are flagged accordingly; smaller on-chain TVLs (Symmetry ~$483K, AMKT ~$350–425K) are DeFiLlama/aggregator figures and some appear stale or dormant.
- **AMKT, Symmetry, EqualFi and Tilt are early/small/uncertain.** AMKT is technically live but effectively dormant (~$350–425K market cap, near-zero volume, 2026). Tilt appears to be a Robinhood-L2 testnet AI-hedge-fund-vault platform, not a NAV/ETF index protocol — a possible name mismatch with the brief's reference. EdenFi is a likely name collision (an African USDC remittance wallet), unrelated to indices.
- **No quantified weekend premium/discount % was found in a primary source.** The reported drop in weekend volume to roughly 30% of weekday levels (PANews) and "ghost price" risk are documented qualitatively; a hard weekend de-peg magnitude would require pulling a Dune/RWA.xyz time series. The "NVDAx ~80% slippage on a $1M redemption" figure comes from a secondary analysis (WEEX) and should be treated as illustrative, not primary.
- **Robinhood Chain is treated as a venue, not a precedent** per the brief; its tokenized-equity tickers are not yet live on mainnet (testnet processed ~4M transactions in its first week, Feb 2026).
- **Mirror/Synthetix are cited as failed precedents** to mark the synthetic-equity register as a graveyard, not as live comparables.
